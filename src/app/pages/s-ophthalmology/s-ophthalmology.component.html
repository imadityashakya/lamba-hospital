At Lamba Multi-Speciality Hospital, our Ophthalmology Department is dedicated to providing comprehensive eye care, ensuring that your vision remains clear, healthy, and well-protected. Using the latest diagnostic and surgical techniques, our team of expert ophthalmologists delivers personalized treatment plans for patients of all ages.

Our Sub-Divisions & Services
Comprehensive Eye Examinations

Regular eye check-ups are crucial for early detection of vision problems and eye diseases. Our detailed eye examinations assess visual acuity, eye pressure, corneal health, and retinal condition to ensure the best eye care for every patient.

Glaucoma Diagnosis and Management

Glaucoma is a silent thief of sight, and early detection is key. Our specialists use non-contact tonometry and OCT scans to monitor eye pressure and optic nerve health, offering both medication-based and laser-assisted treatments to control the disease and prevent vision loss.

Pediatric Ophthalmology

Children require specialized eye care to address conditions like:

  • Lazy eye (Amblyopia)
  • Squint (Strabismus)
  • Refractive errors and congenital eye diseases

Our pediatric eye specialists use child-friendly diagnostic techniques and offer vision therapy and corrective treatments tailored for young patients.

Cataract Surgery using Phacoemulsification

We specialize in advanced cataract removal using phacoemulsification technology, which ensures:

  • Painless, stitch-less surgery
  • Quick recovery and improved vision within days
  • High-quality intraocular lenses (IOLs) for enhanced clarity
Advanced Technology & Equipment
OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography)

High-resolution imaging for detecting retinal diseases, diabetic retinopathy, and macular degeneration.

Phacoemulsification Machine

Ensures safe and precise cataract removal with minimal recovery time.

Non-Contact Tonometer

A painless way to measure intraocular pressure, crucial for glaucoma screening.

Slit Lamp Biomicroscope

Enables detailed examination of the anterior and posterior segments of the eye.
